PaleoNerd 101

By Kate Flann

Get started becoming a Paleonerd by learning 5 quick facts:

  1. Not everything you think is a dinosaur is a dinosaur
  2. The velociraptors in Jurrasic Park are inaccurate.., or are they?
  3. Spinosaurus
  4. TRex is not the best theropod... but also, yes, it is.
  5. Chickens are not ancestors of dinosaurs... they ARE dinosaurs.

What is and is not a dinosaur?

A common misconception of the public is that everything shown in Jurrasic Park or The Land Before Time was a dinosaur. It's an easy mistake to make. Big old creature from dinosaur time? === Dinosaur. However, that just isn't true. Take for example, pterosaurs. They are not in fact dinosaurs as they do not share the same common dinosaur ancestor. Additionally, many seas creatures were not dinosaurs either, including Mosasaur (the giant whale like creature fromm the sea show in Jurrasic World) and Megalodon (the giant shark creature). On that note, did you know that Sharks are older than Dinosaurs? Dinosaurs first appeared some 230 million years ago, while the first sharks appeared some 450 million years ago. Not only are sharks older than dinosaurs- they are older than trees (some 380 million years old)!

Velociraptors

Speaking of Jurrasic Park, for many impressionable youth, Jurrasic Park is their first introduction into the (cool) world of dinosaurs. This has led to many spinoff francises and discussions about the original film and it's accuracies and inaccuracies. When it was first made, Jurrasic Park was actually very true to what we knew about dinosaurs at the time (save for some creative liberties, like the venom spitting, frilled Dilophosaurus who we knew did not have such features). One of the inaccuracies that people love to point out is the wild difference between the Velociraptors in Jurrasic Park and what we know about Velociraptors today, which are closer in size to a turkey. You can see in the infographic below the comical difference in size and appearance.

While many casual people may have heard this, what Paleonerds know is that isn't the full story! At the time of the first movie (1993), paleontologists didn't have as many fossils as we do today. The fossils which they called velociraptor were actually a group of fossils of many different kinds of theropods (carnivourus dinosaurs) closely related to velociraptor, including deinonychus, an 11 foot long predator whose name literally translates to "terrible claw".

Now that sounds more like a Jurrisc Park velociraptor, doesn't it? While the velociraptors in Jurrasic Park are a bit larger than deinonychus, it is most likely that they were based off of deinonychus rather than what we today call velociraptor. That said, they are all awesome dinosaurs who do indeed hunt in packs and are very intelligent creatures. My personal favorite is Utahraptor. What's yours?

Tyrannosaurus Rex

Meet Sue, one of the best and most recent full scale replicas of a Trex. She is currently on display at the Field Museum of Natural History in Chicago. What makes her special is she is one of the largest, most extensive, and best preserved Tyrannosaurus rex specimens ever found, at over 90 percent recovered by bulk. We actually know a LOT about Trex, from their incredible eyesight (which Jurassic Park certainly got wrong), that their young were likely at least partially feathered, and that their vocalizations probably sounded less like a tiger roar and more like a gigantic bellowing crocodile.

We have also learned a lot about a lot of other species of dinosaur too. Trex was one of the first theropod fossils ever categorically discovered and was thusly named "King of the Lizards", but that name is incorrect for several reasons. For example, we now know that dinosaurs were not reptiles or lizards, but an entirely different type of animal. And second, we now know of many other theropods that are much bigger and stronger than Trex. For example, the afforementioned Spinosaurus is actually bigger than Trex in both height and length. As for stronger, Allosaurus, while not bigger than Trex, reportedly was much stronger and was able to take down pray twice the size of Trex. As a Paleonerd, it's important to know this as, claiming Trex to be the "strongest" or "biggest" carnivore will likely set of a chorus of other nerds echoing "that's not true!". All that said, while Trex may not be the biggest, one thing most of us paleonerds agree on is Trex is still the **BEST**. With a stronger bite force than any animal that has ever lived and a legend and icon of dinosauria ever since it's discovery in 1905, for many people around the world, Trex holds a special place in their hearts, and that is why Trex is the best dinosaur.

Chickens and Dinosaurs

You may have snidely heard from somewhere that chickens are the ancestors of the mighty trex. To be clear, this just isn't true. Chickens did not evolve from Trex. And birds did not evolve from dinosaurs... birds ARE dinosaurs.

In the same way that cats did not evolve from tigers but instead evolved from some common feline ancestor over 6 million years ago, birds and dinosaurs like Trex and Tricerotops, all evolved from the same dinosaur common ancestor. In other words, birds didn't evolve from some trex, but rather, birds ancestors lived alongside other theropods during the Triassic period. It's sad sometimes to think about the fact we will never get to see those amazing animals in person- you will never meet a tricerotops or a stegosaurus or a trex (though, maybe that last one is for the best...). But when you truly observe birds, you can see it in the way that they walk and the shape of their feet, that they clearly are dinosaurs walking among us.

And congratulations! Whether you like it or not, you are now a paleonerd.
